## Contents Summary

### B.1 Rapid Visual Screening (RVS) — Pages 225–231 (PDF 252–258)

**B.1.1 Quezon City (QC) Building Classification**

Describes field observations during the January 2018 RVS of QC public school buildings. Key typology notes:

- **"Semi-Concrete":** Typically RCMRFs (C1-L or C1-M) with CHB (concrete hollow block) infill; some with wooden interior/exterior walls or timber flooring.
- **"Prefab" (prefabricated):** Generally 1-storey, 1–3 rooms. Subtypes:
  - Light metal frames (S3-L) with CHB infill
  - RCMRF with CHB infill (potentially pre-cast RC frames, PC2-L)
  - CHB structures (CHB-L), often labelled "Canteen"; possibly pre-cast tilt-up (PC1-L)
- **"Fil-Chinese":** Buildings donated by the Filipino Chinese Chamber of Commerce; one S3-L, one C1-L or possible PC2-L.
- **"Makeshift":** Informal structures (UPD-ICE N-L typology) — light wood frames with G.I. sheets, tarpaulin, plywood, and CHB (Bautista M. L. et al., 2014).
- **"Gabaldon":** Historical building type; two subtypes:
  - Wooden frame and wall (W1-L)
  - RCMRF with wooden upper storey (CWS-L)

**B.1.2 Makati City:** Not a separate subsection; Makati RVS observations are referenced in the Makati inventory processing section (B.2.1).

### B.3 RVS Results — Pages 240–243 (PDF 267–270)

**B.3.1 FEMA P-154 Scoring — Makati City**
- 55 of 96 buildings screened; 34 buildings had structural or detailed architectural drawings; FEMA P-154 scores extrapolated for remaining 11 buildings.
- Most common vertical irregularity: "short column" modifier (CHB infill) applied to all 96 buildings.
- 93 buildings had "Redundancy" modifiers beneficially applied (>2 bays in each direction).
- 2 buildings affected by "Pounding" (level 2) modifier (separation < 1.5% height of shorter building, with one building ≥ 2 storeys taller).
- Result: 53 buildings fall below the recommended cut-off score of 2.0 for detailed evaluation.

**B.3.2 FEMA P-154 Scoring — Quezon City**
- Smaller proportion screened vs. Makati (wider geographic spread of campuses).
- 23 buildings with potential pounding issues (more than Makati).
- Short column irregularities: 94 buildings; Redundancy modifier: 101 buildings.
- Reentrant corner irregularities extrapolated for unscreened buildings via site development plans.
- CHB-L and N-L archetypes required HAZUS-equivalent approximations (Pre-Code URM-L and W1-L, respectively).

**Note:** The latest Philippine Earthquake Model (PEM2020) was used for the seismicity criterion ("High" or "Very High") in developing FEMA P-154 scores.
